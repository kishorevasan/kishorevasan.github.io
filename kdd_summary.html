Network Embedding at KDD

  • by:Kishore Vasan
  • date:August 24, 2018

This summer, I had the opportunity to attend the 24th ACM SIGKDD Conference on Knowledge Discovery and Data mining (KDD). The event this year took place in London from Aug 18 – Aug 23. This year’s KDD was the biggest ever with over 4k attendees and about 1500 paper submissions for research and applied data science tracks.

I was one of the co-authors to a paper titled “Is together better? Examining scientific collaboration across multiple authors, departments and institutions.” accepted to BigScholar workshop at KDD. The workshop discussed on novel methods to analyze and mine large scale scholarly data.

Data is growing at a very past pace. Identifying ways to efficiently embed, visualize, and analyze data in a temporal network form is of need in data mining.” – gist of most of the talks this year at KDD.

Most of the papers this year were focused on temporal network embeddings. There were a couple of papers in this topic that caught my eye:

  • SpotLight: Detecting Anomalies in Streaming Graphs. Combined effort by teams at Carnegie Mellon University and Amazon. The paper introduced a method to detect anomalies by looking at the sudden (dis)appearance of large dense subgraphs. Given snapshots of the network in real time, we can convert the network into embeddings in a vector space and identify if a certain subgraph appears or disappears from a network. This paper introduced the power of network embeddings to detect anomalies.
  • Concepts-Bridges: Uncovering Conceptual Bridges Based on Biomedical Concept Evolution. Work by a team from State University of New York. This paper was particularly interesting to me because they used temporal dynamics infused with word embeddings on biomedical scholarly data to identify concept growth. For example, given two topics of interest “Migraine” (a disease) and “Magnesium” (a mineral), the algorithm can identify “serotonin” (a drug) as a meaningful connection between the two by looking at the local and global transformation of the semantics of the words. This paper introduced a method that is useful in identifying and accelerating scientific growth.

    • Overall, KDD was an experience of a lifetime where I got to learn a lot on variety of novel data mining methods, converse with leading data scientists on trending research questions, and understand the big picture goal of the field. I cannot wait to attend the conference again next year!
